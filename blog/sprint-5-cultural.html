Reflections on Sprint 5

What surprised me the most about learning core is...

I thought I had a fairly good grasp on many of these concepts but I was still able to learn. Being prepared initially with the information on mindset and how you can actually open yourself up to learn anything has really helped me through this process. I’ve been struggling more and more since week three (although I had a real panic doing week one at first, not GitHub is my friend ha!) and being able to go back to that idea that I can and will get it (eventually) is very grounding.

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

That I can be very hard on myself without knowing it. I guess it's ego that makes me feel like I should be able to understand something perfectly (by now) before I can move on and even attempt the next problem. This was really hard for me over the week 5 content because I had a mental battle to really work through first and this has stopped me from actually putting code down or just trying to see what I could achieve. I keep having to remind myself that, yes you don’t know everything, how could you? It’s been three weeks! But that doesn’t mean you can’t attempt something.

Also asking for help makes me feel a bit silly sometimes or like I’ve not grasped something enough. Like I should know it before asking for help. Learning code or any new language is tough stuff.

What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

To know who you are, how you learn, how you react under pressure or in tough situations is a great skill to have because then you can recognize when you are getting to in your head. This helps you to recognize what sort of help or assistance you might need to push yourself forward. Being able to understand these things about yourself also helps you to become more aware of those around you. Being able to have empathy for others who are struggling with something you went through, or just being able to recognize that someone needs an ear is so helpful. It also give you the ability to talk something through (potentially) and come up with a team solution.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

How much I enjoyed it.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

At the beginning asking friends/family about who they think I am (strengths, weaknesses) was a bit uncomfortable for me haha. But it helped me to take a look in at myself and know that the journey I was about to undertake I could do it even if I was going to struggle through it.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

You can’t put a price on being aware, understanding, and genuinely helpful and empathetic to those around you. Also, checking your ego now will save you in a work environment (if you haven’t already gone through this process). Not everyone grows up in an environment where these values are upheld or taught and to have it be a part of a learning environment that you are going through along with your peers is great.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practicing programming instead? Justify your answer.

I don’t think it was a waste. To be honest this should be something that is a part of every level of learning to help build more well rounded humans. I do wish I had more time with the basics of code though but that’s for a different time.

Core 5 Part 2 - Reflecting On Your Learning

My short term goal is to be able to actually grasp JavaScript by the end of this course to the point where I could teach someone. At the moment I am still learning and struggling through the concepts and being able to put them together to make code work.

My long term goal is to gain enough skills that I could either combine code with my current field or move into an entirely different field. I would like the opportunity to be able to work from home and to have clients on the side as some extra income. I do not have an industry in mind at the moment that I am wanting to get into but we’ll see. I might freelance or get a company job. I am hoping to have the ability to have more flexibility in my life by upskilling.

Strengths - communicating and seeing the big picture. I can organise, plan and delegate.

Limitations - technically getting it all together (the “how do we make this work?”). I still feel like I am running before I can walk. Mentally sometimes I am finding that I am limiting myself before exhausting all of my options on certain tasks. I am hoping that being in a classroom/team environment will help more with my learning because I can talk to people face to face.

Staying on top of the material and keeping focused for 9 weeks will be a challenge for me I think. It will be interesting to come back to this at the end of the bootcamp to reflect on how I did. This might be something that I find I need to do further work at the end of the course before reaching out for a job, I don’t know. For me I know I have a hard time moving forward before I understand what I just did and that is something that I am finding that I am struggling with in foundations and will have to work on during bootcamp. I will also ask the same question 10 times until I get it.

Communication, pairing, presenting, further developing my leadership skills, giving and receiving feedback and being consistent with my reflective skills. Everything there is really important to keep developing and improving for me. I don’t think these are skills anyone ever ‘masters’ because you can always work on other ways to improve yourself and therefore be a more effective person to work with. Also just working on how to change my approach to work with whomever I am with. Sometimes you have to change up your style or communication to work with others so being able to understand how someone works before we deep dive into a task together will be key for me I think.

I expect to finish the course with more confidence than I do now. Making sure I am reflective, especially on the things I don’t understand fully so I can make sure to seek help in those areas. Sleep enough and drink water, eat healthy. Having a 5 min meditation before and at the end of the day. I’ve also found that a mid-day session helps sometimes too if I am really wound up. If you don’t understand something you need to do some research and then ask for help or an explanation. Stop just trying to work through it on your own. This means dropping your ego regularly. Cry if you need to then start again.
